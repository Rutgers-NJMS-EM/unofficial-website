Stephen Alerhand, M.D.

Assitant Professor, EM: Fellowship Director

Dr. Alerhand completed both his Emergency Medicine Residency and Emergency Ultrasound Fellowship at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. During this time, he was bestowed local and national awards in ultrasound education.

He has published numerous peer-reviewed studies, reviews, and book chapters in Emergency Ultrasound. He also operates the US Probes section for the EMdocs.net FOAM site that receives up to 9,000 views per day.

Nationally, Dr. Alerhand serves as Co-Chair of the ACEP Ultrasound Residency Education Committee. Internationally, he is the Director of the Annual Critical Care Ultrasound Workshop for the Israeli Association of Emergency Medicine.

Ilya Ostrovsky, M.D.

Assitant Professor, EM

Dr. Ostrovsky received his medical degree and completed residency training as Chief Resident at Rutgers NJMS, and then stayed on as faculty. After residency, he finished the Ultrasound Leadership Academy and then the Resuscitation Leadership Academy.

He has a passion for emergency ultrasound, and his true interest lies in the incorporation of ultrasound into the field of critical care.

Dr. Ostrovsky loves to teach and was not only awarded by the residents with the annual Attending of the Year Award, but recently promoted to Assistant Program Director.

Annette Mueller, M.D., M.B.A.

Assistant Professor, EM

Dr. Mueller was born in Altdorf, Bavaria, and studied economics in both Germany and France. Acquiring her MBA from Harvard, she then decided to transition to her ultimate dream of Medicine.

After completeing her pre-med/bost-bacc at Columbia University, she then attended SUNY Downstate Medical School and completed her Emergency Medicine Residency at Kings County/SUNY Downstate Medical Center.

A series of firsts, after being first-ever fellow and graduate of this Ultrasound fellowship, Dr. Mueller is now also the first official recruited faculty member of the Ultrasound Program at Rutgers University.
